Defending Wild Washington

Defending Wild Washington: A Citizen's Action Guide was an ambitious project I joined for my junior year at The Evergreen State College. Seventeen students and two professors had a school-year to conduct and analyze in depth research and collaborate to write an extensive overview of the conservation movement in Washington State. My efforts included co-authoring Chapter 2: A History of Washington's Landscape Transformations, and Chapter 4: Riding the Wave. In chapter 2, I detailed the environmental changes Euro-Americans made to Washington's landscape and in chapter 4, I gave an account of the (then) current socioeconomic conditions of the state and how they could effect the conservation movement.
